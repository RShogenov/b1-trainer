05

MVP Scope — What to Build

Build in v1 (4–6 weeks)

  • Student & family profiles Name, contact, instrument, level, emergency notes
  • Recurring lesson scheduling Weekly / biweekly with per-family exceptions (holidays, summer)
  • True two-way Google Calendar sync Trojan horse — zero migration required, teacher keeps their current calendar
  • Bulletproof billing engine Monthly or per-lesson. Handles cancellations, makeups, prorations, late fees — always accurate
  • Card-on-file autopay Parent enters card once at onboarding. Never manually pays again.
  • Policy onboarding flow Parent signs cancellation policy digitally. System enforces it. Teacher stays silent.
  • Make-up credit tracker Tracks credits owed, expiry dates, auto-notifies parent 1 week before expiry
  • Parent portal (web, no app) View upcoming lessons, past invoices, update card. Just a URL.

Skip in v1 — save for v2

  • Progress notes / lesson content tracking Nice to have. Not the core pain.
  • Website hosting My Music Staff does this. Not the reason anyone switches.
  • Multi-teacher / staff management Teachworks territory. Out of solo teacher scope.
  • Marketing / lead capture tools Teachers find students via word-of-mouth. Not a workflow pain.
  • Mobile native app PWA / mobile web is sufficient for v1. Teachers are at a desk.
  • Group lesson / recital management Edge case. Solve the 1:1 lesson workflow first.

2-Week Validation Plan (Zero Code)

Week 1 · Days 1–3 — Listen
Confirm the pain
  • Post in r/musicteachers: "What's your #1 billing headache?" — no pitch
  • Post in PianoWorld Teacher Forum — same question
  • Join 2–3 music teacher Facebook groups and lurk
  • Read every 1-star review of MMS, TutorBird, and Fons on Capterra
Week 1 · Days 4–7 — Build the list
Collect demand signals
  • Set up a one-page landing site with email capture only
  • Headline: "Studio billing that parents never question"
  • No screenshots needed — just the promise and an email box
  • Post it in communities as a "building in public" update
Week 2 · Days 8–14 — Validate intent
Turn interest into money signals
  • DM 20 teachers who engaged in pain-point threads
  • Offer first 10 signups a lifetime deal at $9.99/mo (lock-in for early adopters)
  • Do 5 discovery calls — ask about their current tool, their worst billing story
  • Optional: sell a $19 "No-Drama Billing Kit" Notion template on Gumroad first

Why This Scored 8.5 / 10

8.5/10
  • 500k–700k solo practitioners in the US — far larger than official numbers show
  • Paid professionals who own their own $20/month decision. No committee. No boss.
  • Incumbents (MMS, TutorBird) have identical, documented, unfixed billing bugs from 2010 to today
  • No dominant, well-funded, beloved player. Fragmented mediocre SaaS — not TeamSnap with VC money.
  • Customer acquisition is free: concentrated communities (PianoWorld, teacher Facebook groups)
  • Google Calendar sync is a Trojan horse — zero migration friction for new users
  • Low churn expected: teachers who set up their studio stay for years. Students are long-term.
Main risk
Volume required
At $20/mo, reaching $1M ARR needs 4,200 customers. Low price = volume game. Needs consistent community-driven acquisition.
Mitigation
Add tutors too
Academic tutors (SAT prep, language, math) have identical billing problems. Same product, 3× larger market.
Expansion path
Studio plan at $49
When a teacher grows to 3+ staff, offer a multi-teacher tier. Natural upsell path without rebuilding the product.
